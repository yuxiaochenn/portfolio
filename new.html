Overview

Walmart.com uses an address verification service (AVS) to ensure our customer’s entered address is registered with major shipping carriers to reduce transaction frictions. The system auto-corrects minor errors or prompts suggested address(es) on a validator to minimize customer’s efforts in proceeding with checkout.

Problems on Existing Address Validator

Through secondary research (Baymard report) and guerrilla testing, we found the validator lacks clarification of 1) the cause of the validation error and 2) options for customers to effectively override incorrect suggestions by AVS.

Re-design Goals

  • Create affordance to prompt customers using a suggested address
  • Indicate options to handle AVS edge case - override incorrect suggestions by either editing or continuing with their original input
  • Refine design and context to clarify the cause of the validation status

Updated Primary and Secondary Address Validation Flows

Red Flag in Guest Customer Flow

I realized the proposed design wasn’t perfect for guest customer scenario. As an inpage section, the validator overwrote the previous form and its affiliated CTAs, which caused the main navigation became out of sight and sowed the seeds of violating a key pattern in checkout flow.

After another round of exploration and team critique, I realized inpage validator wouldn't be the one-size-fits-all solution.

Further Iteration

Inpage - Drawer - Modal

The final proposal turned the validator from an in-page section into a modal, which was a scalable solution for both user scenarios.
