Optimizing Disclosure on Estimated Taxes and Fees for Walmart Customers

Shipped Project · End-to-End Experience Design · Responsive Web · Native Apps

My Role

  • Drove UI/UX design from concept to launch
  • Cross-functional collaboration with Product Manager, Content Strategist, and Design Researcher

Overview

Walmart.com displays an estimated amount of taxes and fees in purchase order summary (POS) as a heads-up to help customers manage budget on the total cost of their order during transaction. However, sales tax and associated fees depend on more factors than a Geotargeted zip code; therefore the accurate amount will only be calculated with a confirmed fulfillment address.

To improve the customer’s perception of trust, we optimized the disclosure of taxes and fees by hiding the estimated amount before the tax call.

Red Flag on Existing Design

Although we noticed positive lift in GMV and conversion rate during an A/B testing, there’s space for further improvement on the usability.

Presence of Zip Code with Estmated Tax Hidden

With a zip code attached to the tax line, customers believed that's all needed to estimate their sales taxes and fees. They were confused why it's hidden, especially given the capability to manually update the zip code.

Learnings from Other eCommerce Apps

  • When a site decides to hide estimated tax before it's finalized, a common approach is to remove the sense of location from POS.
  • To clarify the intention of hiding estimated tax, attaching a disclaimer to POS is a widely adopted approach.

Evaluating the Impact of Removing Zip Code Link

Before jumping directly into design, I conducted a use case audit and confirmed the impact of removing zip code from the transaction flow would be minimal. On top of a low CTR on the zip code link in POS, the main use cases required location update to proceed transaction have been handled explicitly in the existing design.

Design Explorations on Disclaimer Displacement

I explored multiple options to display the disclaimer in POS to communicate when taxes and fees will be disclosed. We decided to move forward with attaching a disclaimer to the tax line, with a certainty of customers' understanding of "- -" and lowered interaction cost.

Final Solution

I worked with our content strategist to make the disclaimer message more explicit with end-to-end transaction flows mapped out. The final solution got buy-in from cross-functional team successfully.

What I learned

I never thought working on a project with such a small scope would be this rewarding. From pixels and font sizes to choose every single word, I truly realized the incredible efforts need to be weaved into a thoughtful design decision to bring a solid checkout experience to our 100+ million monthly active customers.

The Impact

In May 2020, we rolled out the new design and received significant gains. We achieved a $58.2m lift in gross merchandise volume with a 1.6% growth in visitor conversion combining desktop and mobile visits.
